## NHL Strategy & Theory — Betting-Relevant Concepts

### Possession Metrics

**Corsi (CF%)** — Shot attempt differential (all blocked + missed + on-target shots)
- CF% > 50% = team controls possession, spends more time in offensive zone
- Teams with top Corsi in 2023-24 all posted 99+ points and made playoffs
- Betting use: Fade teams with CF% below 45% at home; lean on teams above 55%

**Fenwick (FF%)** — Unblocked shot attempt differential (removes blocked shots)
- More predictive than Corsi for future performance
- Teams that block lots of shots can inflate Corsi; Fenwick is cleaner

**Expected Goals (xG)** — Shot quality model weighting angle, distance, shot type, pre-shot movement
- High-danger xG generates from slot and crease areas
- xG% > actual GF% = team is "better than their record" (buy)
- xG% < actual GF% = team "got lucky" (fade before regression)
- Best source: **MoneyPuck.com** or **Natural Stat Trick**

### Zone Entry Theory

**Controlled entries** (carry-in) vs **Dump-and-chase** entries:
- Controlled entries generate **2-3x more scoring chances** than dump-ins
- Teams with high controlled entry rates are analytically superior even if their record doesn't show it
- Lean on teams with high control% against teams that rely on dump-and-chase
- Betting edge: When a speed/skill team faces a big physical team, the skilled team's entry efficiency often compensates for the size mismatch

### Last Change Advantage (Home Ice)

**Critical for NHL betting:**
- Home team puts lines on ice SECOND — gets to match favorable lines
- Away team must commit first, home coach then deploys ideal counter-matchup
- Elite coaches (Brind'Amour, Cooper, Bednar) exploit this relentlessly
- Betting implication: Home teams with strong depth coaching gain extra edge vs road teams with one dominant line

### Systems Reference

| System | Teams Using It | Betting Implication |
|--------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Defensive/Low-event** | CAR, BUF, COL, PIT, DET, LAK | Lean UNDER, lower totals |
| **High-event/Rush** | NJD (Keefe), NSH (Brunette), MTL | Lean OVER, higher goals |
| **Forecheck-heavy** | CAR, TBL, FLA | Tilts ice — lean home team |
| **Analytics-driven** | COL, TBL, CAR, VGK | Efficient, hard to exploit |
| **Veteran defensive** | BUF (Ruff), CBJ/WPG (Bowness) | Low event, lean under |

### Divisional Game Adjustments

- Division rivals play each other 4x per season + potential playoff matchups
- Coaches have extensive film on division opponents — games tend to be tighter
- Large spreads against division opponents rarely hold → **Division Dog archetype fires more reliably**
- Playoff series: Coaches make major adjustments after Game 1; line 2 of a series is often sharper than Game 1

### Special Teams — Betting Edge

**Power Play (PP%) vs Penalty Kill (PK%) Mismatch:**
- PP% 20%+ vs PK% 75% or lower = significant edge (Archetype #42)
- Late in period draws are when top PP units deploy — can create over value on totals
- Teams that take many penalties against strong PP units are at structural disadvantage

**Key coaching PP tendencies:**
- Jon Cooper (TBL): Among the best PP designers in NHL history
- Rod Brind'Amour (CAR): Elite PK — suppresses opponent PP impact
- John Tortorella (VGK): Physically demanding — teams take more penalties, which can go either way

## Hockey Theory — Advanced Concepts for Betting

### xG (Expected Goals) — The Most Powerful NHL Betting Tool

**What it measures:** Probability of each shot being a goal, based on:
- Shot location (slot vs perimeter)
- Shot type (wrist, snap, slap, deflection)
- Pre-shot movement (pass before shot = higher xG)
- Traffic/screens in front

**How to use it for betting:**
- Team with higher xGF% (expected goals for) is likely to win more than their record shows
- If xGF% > actual GF% → team is unlucky, expect positive regression → BUY them as dogs
- If xGF% < actual GF% → team has been shooting above average → FADE them as favorites

**Best sources:** MoneyPuck.com, Natural Stat Trick, Hockey-Reference

### Corsi + Fenwick — Possession Proxy

| Metric | CF% Range | What It Means |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| CF% > 55% | Elite possession team | Strong betting favorite regardless of record |
| CF% 50-55% | Good possession team | Slight edge in close games |
| CF% 45-50% | Below average | Volume shots against; opponents create more |
| CF% < 45% | Poor possession | Significant structural disadvantage |

**Betting use:** When a CF% 55%+ home team faces a CF% 45%- road team, possession mismatch creates real edge beyond what odds reflect.

### Goalie Variance — Key Principle

**Goalies are the highest-variance position in NHL betting.** The same shot quality can produce wildly different outcomes depending on goalie performance. 

Key facts:
- SV% variance is enormous game-to-game — even elite goalies have bad nights
- **Backup goalie games** are among the highest-value betting spots in sports
- When an elite goalie (SV% .920+) faces a backup (.890-), the xG gap is significant
- **Goalie bounce-back** (Archetype #39): After allowing 4+ goals, goalies historically rebound sharply next start

### Scheduling and Travel — NHL-Specific

| Situation | Impact | Betting |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| 3rd game in 4 nights | Significant fatigue, NHL pace wears down players | Lean against this team (Archetype #43) |
| 5th game in 7 nights (road) | Extreme fatigue | Strong lean against (Archetype #44) |
| Road back-to-back | Less severe than NBA but real | Minor lean against B2B road team |
| Altitude (Denver) | Tires players faster, affects conditioning | Road teams at COL on short rest struggle more |

### Division Games — NHL-Specific Edge

- **Division rivals know each other** — coaches have full film packages, systems counter-adjusted
- Division games are historically tighter regardless of record gap
- **Large spreads in division games rarely hold** → Division Dog archetype fires reliably
- In playoffs, first-round division series often go to 6-7 games regardless of seeding
