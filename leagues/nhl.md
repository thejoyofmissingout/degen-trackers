## NHL Playoff Format & Rules

### Bracket Structure
- 16 teams total (8 per conference)
- Each conference: 3 division winners + 1 wild card per division = 8 per conference
- **Division winners seed 1-3 within each conference**
- **Wild cards are seeded as WC1 and WC2 — they always play the top seed in their division**
- Wild card team's conference record determines WC1 vs WC2

### Series Format
- All rounds: Best-of-7
- Home ice: Higher seed hosts games 1, 2, 5, 7; lower seed hosts 3, 4, 6
- Overtime: Sudden death (5-on-5, no shootout) — periods continue until goal scored

### Tiebreaker Rules (for standings)
1. **Points percentage** (if unequal games played)
2. **Regulation wins (RW)** — wins NOT in OT/SO — this is #1 when games played are equal
3. **Regulation + Overtime wins (ROW)** — excludes shootout wins
4. **Total wins** (W column)
5. **Head-to-head points** among tied clubs
6. **Goal differential** (capped)
7. **Total goals scored**

⚠️ **Critical:** Regulation wins are the first tiebreaker. A team with more OT/SO wins than actual regulation wins is vulnerable in tiebreakers. LA Kings' weakness (only 20 RW) is a real concern if Nashville catches them on points.
