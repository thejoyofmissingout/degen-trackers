# Injury Recovery Timelines & Load Management — All Sports
*Last updated: 2026-04-14*

---

## HOW TO USE THIS DOC

Before placing any bet involving a key player being out or limited:
1. **Identify the injury type** from the official injury report
2. **Cross-reference the timeline** below to estimate return window
3. **Check the load management section** — is this a rest situation, not an injury?
4. **Apply the line adjustment rule** — books often under-react to late injury news

### NBA — Common Injuries

| Injury | Severity | Typical Recovery | Betting Signal |
|--------|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Ankle sprain** | Grade 1 (mild) | 1–2 weeks | If key player out, lean against that team |
| **Ankle sprain** | Grade 2 (moderate) | 3–6 weeks | Significant line impact; fade team |
| **Ankle sprain** | Grade 3 (severe/tear) | 6–12 weeks | Season-altering; major fade |
| **Hamstring strain** | Grade 1 | 1–2 weeks | Short term; monitor day-to-day |
| **Hamstring strain** | Grade 2 | 3–4 weeks | Miss 10–15 games |
| **Hamstring strain** | Grade 3 (tear) | 6–12 weeks | Near-certain IL |
| **Knee — MCL sprain** | Mild | 2–4 weeks | Monitor; often plays through |
| **Knee — MCL sprain** | Moderate/severe | 4–8 weeks | Multiple games missed |
| **Knee — ACL tear** | Full tear (surgery) | 9–12 months | Season over |
| **Knee — ACL tear** | Partial (no surgery) | 3 months | Out for significant stretch |
| **Achilles tendon** | Rupture | 9–12 months | Season over, often impacts following season too |
| **Foot/plantar fascia** | Varies | 2–8 weeks | Nagging; reduces explosiveness even when active |
| **Back** | Tightness | Day-to-day | Fade if questionable |
| **Back** | Disc issue | 4–8 weeks | Significant absence |
| **Concussion** | Mild | 7–14 days, 1–2 games | League protocol: must clear testing |
| **Concussion** | Moderate/severe | 2–6+ weeks | Unpredictable; teams are cautious |
| **Shoulder** | Dislocation/labrum | 4–8 weeks | Reduces shooting/finishing |
| **Illness** | Flu/illness | 1–3 days | Same-day disclosure; fade if listed |

**NBA Injury Betting Rules:**
- Star announced OUT same day = underreacted line; bet against that team early
- "Questionable" for stars = 50/50 but lean against; books don't fully price uncertainty
- "Doubtful" = ~75% sitting; bet as if out
- "Day-to-day" = monitor through tip; live betting edge if they sit and line hasn't moved

### NHL — Common Injuries

*Note: NHL teams notoriously vague — only disclose "upper body" (UBI) or "lower body" (LBI)*

| Injury / Designation | Typical Recovery | Betting Signal |
|---------------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Upper body — mild** | 1–2 weeks (5–7 games) | Missing 1 game to 2 weeks |
| **Upper body — moderate** | 2–4 weeks | Significant; especially impacts top lines |
| **Upper body — severe** | 4–8+ weeks | Long-term absence; roster adjusts |
| **Lower body — mild** | 1–2 weeks | Hard to play through; usually sits |
| **Lower body — moderate** | 3–6 weeks | Multiple weeks out |
| **Lower body — severe (knee)** | 6–12 weeks | Major absence |
| **Shoulder — AC joint (Grade I)** | Days to 2 weeks | Often plays through |
| **Shoulder — AC joint (Grade II)** | 3–6 weeks | Probable game time missed |
| **Shoulder — AC joint (Grade III+)** | 2–3 months | Major absence, possible surgery |
| **Knee — MCL sprain** | 2nd most common injury | 2–6 weeks depending on grade |
| **Knee — ACL tear** | Surgery needed | Season over (9–12 months) |
| **Concussion** | Median: 6 days, but wide range | 69% miss <10 days; 31% miss 10+ days |
| **Concussion — severe** | Up to 342 days in extreme cases | Unpredictable; team is cautious |
| **Groin/hip flexor** | Very common in hockey stride | 1–3 weeks; recurring risk |
| **Back** | Tightness common in hockey | Day-to-day to 3 weeks |

**NHL-Specific Notes:**
- Teams NEVER specify exact injury location — only UBI or LBI
- "Day-to-day" in NHL = could miss 1 game or 5; check beat reporters (Daily Faceoff, PuckPedia)
- Backup goalie games are the highest-leverage injury impact in hockey — always check starter status before betting
- Goalies often start warm-ups and then get pulled; confirm closer to puck drop
- **Best source for real-time NHL injuries:** [DailyFaceoff](https://www.dailyfaceoff.com/hockey-player-news/injuries/) and [PuckPedia](https://puckpedia.com/injuries)

### MLB — Common Injuries

| Injury | Typical Recovery | IL Designation | Betting Signal |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Oblique strain — Grade 1** | 27 days (hitters), 35 days (pitchers) | 10-day IL minimum | Pitcher out → significant line shift; check ERA of replacement |
| **Oblique strain — Grade 2/3** | 6–8 weeks (up to 3–4 months if surgery) | 15–60 day IL | Major rotation disruption |
| **Hamstring strain — Grade 1** | ~14.5 days average | 10-day IL | Often placed on IL preemptively |
| **Hamstring strain — Grade 2/3** | 4–8 weeks | 15-day IL+ | Extended absence for speedsters/IF |
| **UCL (Tommy John)** | 12–18 months | 60-day IL or season end | Season over for pitcher; 80–87% return in ~15 months |
| **Forearm strain** | 3–6 weeks | 15-day IL | Precursor to UCL issues; monitor carefully |
| **Elbow tightness** | Day-to-day to 2 weeks | 10-day IL | Warning sign for deeper issues — fade pitcher |
| **Shoulder inflammation** | 2–4 weeks | 15-day IL | Recurring risk; ERA impact when returns |
| **Quad strain** | 2–4 weeks | 10-day IL | Mainly affects hitters/baserunning |
| **Knee** | 2–6 weeks (ligament dependent) | 10–15 day IL | Affects hitter power/footwork |
| **Back** | Days to 3 weeks | Day-to-day or 10-day IL | Common in pitchers; velocity/movement affected |
| **Concussion** | 7-day minimum (MLB protocol) | 7-day IL | Fixed minimum — player cannot return early |
| **Calf strain** | 2–3 weeks | 10-day IL | Common; affects catcher/OF movement |

**MLB Injury Betting Rules:**
- **Pitcher on IL = critical** — check the replacement starter's ERA/FIP before betting on that game
- "Elbow tightness" or "forearm tightness" in a pitcher = red flag for something worse; fade that team
- Oblique strains routinely exceed initial timelines — treat 6 weeks as the floor, not ceiling
- Early season obliques (cold April weather, stiff muscles) are extremely common — this is prime IL season

| **Low ankle sprain** | Grade 1–2 | 1–2 weeks | Short-term; skill players fade |
| **Concussion** | Protocol | 1–2 weeks minimum (1 game clearance required) | Automatic 1-week watch |
| **Concussion** | Severe | 2–4+ weeks | Unpredictable; fade until cleared |
| **Shoulder — AC joint** | Grade 1–2 | 1–3 weeks | Often plays through with padding |
| **Shoulder — labrum/rotator cuff** | Surgery needed | 4–6 months | Half-season loss minimum |
| **Rib/pectoral** | Strain | 2–4 weeks | Affects QB throwing, WR routes |
| **Back** | Tightness | Day-to-day | Often plays through; monitor |
| **Turf toe** | Moderate | 3–8 weeks | Underrated; significantly affects explosive players |
| **Foot fracture** | Jones fracture (most common NFL) | 8–12 weeks | Significant absence; often recurs |

**NFL Injury Betting Rules:**
- QB injuries are the single biggest line-mover in sports — starting vs backup QB = 7–14 pt swing
- "Limited" in practice = likely plays but reduced; "DNP" = strong lean to sit
- High ankle sprains always run longer than initial estimates — treat 3 weeks as starting point
- Turf toe is massively underrated as a speed/explosiveness killer even when player "plays through"
- **Official NFL injury report** is legally required; "Questionable" is ~50%, "Doubtful" is ~75% out

## LOAD MANAGEMENT — When Teams Rest Stars (Not Injured)

### NBA Load Management Patterns

**Rule:** NBA star players (All-Star or All-NBA in any of prior 3 seasons) cannot be rested on national TV games. But they absolutely get rested in other scenarios.

| Scenario | Typical Action | Betting Signal |
|----------|---------------|----------------|
| **Team clinched top seed, final week** | Rest 1–3 stars completely | Fade clinched team's last 2–3 games |
| **Long road trip, back-to-back** | Stars often get games off | Check injury report day-of |
| **4th game in 5 nights** | High probability of minutes restriction | Lean against even if "active" |
| **Age 30+ star, minor soreness** | DNP-rest is likely | Same-day announcements are market-movers |
| **Stars already locked into seed** | "Voluntary" DNP | Watch for late scratches |

**2025-26 specific:** On the final Friday of the regular season (April 11), 168 total players sat out. Knicks rested Brunson, Hart, Anunoby, Robinson, AND KAT all at once. **Do not bet on clinched-seed teams the final week without confirming starters.**

**Key load management coaches:**
- High-rest tendency: Gregg Popovich era (San Antonio), Rick Carlisle (IND)
- Low-rest tendency: Teams in play-in fights — they play everyone full minutes

### NHL Rest Patterns

- NHL coaches rarely rest stars in regular season — "load management" is not part of hockey culture
- Exception: teams with seeding locked and back-to-back (especially December/January)
- **Playoff context:** In playoffs, injured players often "play through" things they'd sit with in regular season — inflated return rate, but reduced effectiveness
- Watch for a player listed on the morning skate but not taking full rushes — signals probable out

### MLB Rest Patterns

- Starters get skipped in rotation during off-days (natural rest)
- "Routine rest" for everyday players is common in late September if team is locked in
- Teams clinched in August/September: stars may get 1–2 days off per week
- **Key tell:** Manager mentions "maintaining freshness" or "staying sharp" = voluntary rest coming

### NFL Rest Patterns

- Teams clinched + meaningless Week 18 = massive star resting
- Playoff teams with nothing to play for regularly sit entire starting lineups Week 18
- **Biggest betting trap of the year:** Betting a -7 favorite in Week 18 when they have nothing to gain — bet the huge dog instead

### WNBA Rest Patterns

- Load management uncommon due to short season (44 games) and smaller rosters
- Stars rarely sit unless genuinely injured
- Late-season rest may occur if team has locked in seed — but much less systematic than NBA
- Monitor day-before announcements from team accounts

## LINE ADJUSTMENT RULES

| Timing of News | Market Reaction | Your Edge |
|---------------|----------------|-----------|
| Star out announced 2+ days before | Fully priced in | No edge; take at face value |
| Star out announced day before | Partially priced in | Check if line moved enough |
| Star out announced morning of game | Under-priced usually | Bet against team with absent star |
| Star out announced within 1 hour of tip | Massive edge window | Move fast — books lag by 15–30 min |
| "Questionable" stays questionable | 50/50 uncertainty discount | Live bet if they're out and line doesn't move |
